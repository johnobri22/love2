This term is associated with theatre practitioner Jerzy Grotowski. Grotowski was a Pole, born in 1933. He came of age in the 1960’s, a time when the revolutionary spirit of the era shaped his new ideas about theatre. He is most associated with the term poor theatre. An example of his theory was seen in a production of his of Stanisław Wyspiański‘s Akropolis. Grotowski adapted the setting to 20ty century concentration camps. Throughout the performance the actors built a crematorium from scratch around the audience with its completion coinciding with the end of the play.
